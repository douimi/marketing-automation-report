Universal Documents (5)

Air Waybill

Download an example

This document can be used if no country-specific requirements already exist. A waybill is a document providing for the automatic release of cargo to a named consignee. It is a receipt of goods but not a negotiable instrument and is an evidence of contract of carriage. The use of waybill does not effect the terms of the carriage and the terms of carriage may differ as per the carriers policies.

Commercial Invoice

Download an example

This document can be used if no country-specific requirements already exist. A commercial invoice is a formal demand note for payment, issued by the exporter to the importer for goods sold under a sales contract. The document gives details of the goods sold, payment terms and trade terms. In addition, it is used for the customs clearance of goods and serves as a base for calculating customs duties.

Confirmation of Insurance

Download an example

This document can be used if no country-specific requirements already exist. A confirmation of insurance is a document issued by an insurance company. It confirms that an insurance policy has been purchased. It covers the loss or damage of ships, cargo, terminals, and any transport or cargo by which property is transferred, acquired, or held between the points of origin and final destination. The terms and conditions covered are as per the insurance documents.

Packing List

Download an example

This document can be used if no country-specific requirements already exist. A packing list provides details about the shipment such as number of packages, weight, volume and description of the merchandise. One objective of the packing list is to facilitate the inspection and survey of the consignment that comprises the shipment and is included in the invoice. Custom officials and carriers use the packing list as a check-list to verify the outgoing and incoming cargo.

Proforma Invoice

Download an example

This document can be used if no country-specific requirements already exist. A proforma invoice is a price quote or estimated invoice sent by a seller to a buyer in advance of a delivery of goods. It informs the buyer of the kind and quantity of goods, their value, and other important information such as weight and payment terms. This helps the buyer to make a transaction decision and to confirm the order.

Download an example

Single Administrative Document (SAD)

Download an example

Until the date of the full use of the electronic customs systems, the customs authorities may allow for means other than electronic data-processing techniques to be used in accordance with the Union Customs Code Transitional Delegated Act (UCC TDA) on 17 December 2015. Therefore, the paper-based model for EU customs declarations continues to be the Single Administrative Document (SAD). The SAD is composed of a set of eight copies needed to complete the formalities relating to one or more customs procedures as per Appendix B1 or B2 for computerised printing. Continuation forms are provided in Appendix B3-B4. Consult Appendix C1 for explanatory notes (and relevant national guidance) on how to correctly make use of and complete the SAD.

Source: Commission Delegated Regulation (TDA) (EU) 2016/341 of 17 December 2015

Transit Accompanying Document (TAD)

Download an example

As a general rule Union Transit declarations must be made electronically using the NCTS. Paper declarations are only allowed during fallback. However, until the dates of the upgrading of the NCTS referred to in the Annex to Implementing Decision 2014/255/EU, the paper-based Union transit procedure for goods carried by rail, air or sea as referred may continue to apply under certain conditions. Customs authorities issue the TAD (Transit Accompanying Document) to accompany the goods that are being transported. It contains a bar code and/or a unique Movement Reference Number (MRN) to identify the customs declaration pertaining to it. Additional explanatory notes and particulars (data) for the UCC TDA are available in Appendix F1 of the Union Customs Code Transitional Delegated Act (UCC TDA).

Source: Commission Delegated Regulation (TDA) (EU) 2016/341 of 17 December 2015

Country Level Documents (1)

Single Administrative Document (SAD)

Download an example

The official model for declarations to customs by the normal procedure shall be the Single Administrative Document. The single administrative document is composed of a set of eight copies which have the following functions. Copy 1: Kept by the customs authorities in the country where the export or transit formalities are carried out; Copy 2: Used for statistical purposes by the customs authorities; Copy 3: Returned to the exporter after being stamped by the customs; Copy 4: Retained by the office of destination after completing the operation of transit; Copy 5: Return copy for the transit procedure; Copy 6: Retained by the customs authorities of the destination office where customs formalities are completed; Copy 7: Used for statistical purposes by the customs authorities of the Office of destination (for transit formalities and arrival); Copy 8: Returned to the sender after being sealed by customs authorities.

Source: Albanian Customs
Trade Agreement Documents (1)

Certificate of Origin EUR 1

Download an example

The Certificate of the Country of Origin EUR 1 shall be issued by the customs authorities of the exporting country on application having been made in writing by the exporter or, under the exporter's responsibility, by his authorized representative. For this purpose, the exporter or his authorized representative shall fill out the EUR 1 certificate and the application form, which are later signed and sealed by the customs. The EUR 1 certificate serves only for preferential, which means only if the products are accompanied by the EUR 1 certificate they may profit from the liberalization of the customs duties.

Source: Albania Customs
