Financing Operations

Comprehensive guide to export and import financing solutions

Export Financing/Advances

Description

It is a Credit operation through which the bank advances a determined amount, Euros or any currency with official quotation, to a Spanish exporter, so that they can collect the amount of sales with deferred payment made to a foreign importer.

In this process, the participation of three figures stands out:

  • Financial Institution
  • Spanish Exporter
  • Foreign Importer
Important: Financing must always be linked to an export operation.

Operation Process

Export Financing Process

Financing operation diagram

  1. Existence of a commercial purchase-sale agreement between the Spanish Exporter and the foreign Importer.
  2. The Spanish exporter delivers the goods to the foreign Importer, committing to payment at a future date.
  3. The Financial Institution advances to the Exporter, in Euros or Foreign Currency, the amount of the operation.
  4. Upon maturity, the Importer pays the purchase amount.
  5. The Exporter repays the Advance with the reimbursement from the Importer.

Characteristics of Export Financing/Advances

Contract Term

The term will normally coincide with the payment deferral granted to the importer or service recipient. In case the terms do not coincide, we would speak of two alternatives:

- Pre-financing: The Bank finances the Exporter for the manufacturing of the goods.

- Post-financing (Advance): The Bank advances, totally or partially, the amount of the operation once the goods have been shipped. Generally, the terms in this type of operations do not usually exceed 90 days.

Amount

The maximum amount that can be financed coincides with the value of the goods plus expenses (transport, insurance, etc.) that although initially borne by the Exporter, will be reimbursed by the Importer.

Currency

The currency of the Financing/Advance can be in any currency with official quotation. The currency does not have to coincide with the currency in which the export operation is denominated, although it is advisable that they coincide to avoid exchange rate risk.

Interest Rate

The reference interest rate will depend on the currency in which the operation is financed.

Exchange Rate Risk

When the currency of the Financing/Advance and that of the reimbursement do not coincide, an exchange rate risk is generated before which the Exporter can act in two ways: assume it or cover it by contracting an Exchange Rate Insurance or a Currency Option.

Forfaiting: Financing & Credit Insurance

Through forfaiting, a non-recourse financing product, financial institutions offer their exporting clients the possibility of selling without recourse the commercial credits resulting from their exports. In cases where the importer declares insolvency, the banking entity will not be able to claim the amount from the exporter.

Facilities Offered by Banking Entities through Forfaiting
  • Ensure collection guarantee.
  • Exchange rate and interest rate risk disappear as funds are advanced.
  • Political and commercial risks of the operation are eliminated.
  • Provides liquidity.
  • Allows operations to be taken off balance sheet
  • Allows selling more or at higher prices by offering longer payment terms.

In general terms, this type of operation involves high amounts as well as deferred payments over time, so that the seller's financial institution requests that the banking entity resident in the importer's country guarantee the operation. This condition will be materialized through additional guarantees or by endorsing the instruments used as means of payment for the export.

In forfaiting operations, line concessions are not used, but rather the financial institutions that grant forfaiting operations analyze and grant this type of financing, operation by operation.

Import Financing

Description

It is a Credit operation, through which the bank finances the period necessary for the marketing and collection of the imported product. Consequently, the Importer requests financing for:

  • Lacking funds to pay cash.
  • Obtaining better purchase prices.

It is an operation similar to a domestic Credit, differing in that payments are destined exclusively for import payments.

In this process, the participation of three figures stands out:

  • Financial Institution
  • Spanish Importer
  • Foreign Exporter
Important: Financing must always be linked to the payment of an import.

Operation Process

Import Financing Process

Financing operation diagram

  1. Existence of a commercial purchase/sale agreement between the Spanish Importer and the foreign Exporter.
  2. The foreign Exporter delivers the goods to the Spanish Importer.
  3. The Spanish Importer requests Financing from their Bank so that it pays the Foreign Exporter.
  4. The Spanish Importer's Bank pays the foreign Exporter.
  5. Upon maturity, the Spanish Importer repays the financing with the proceeds from their sales.

Characteristics of Import Financing

Contract Term

The term will coincide with the period necessary for the marketing and collection of the imported product. Generally, the terms in this type of operations do not usually exceed 90 days, although it will depend on the imported product.

Amount

Up to 100% of the operation, even more if the purchase is Incoterm F.O.B.

Currency

The currency of Import Financing can be in any currency with official quotation. The currency does not have to coincide with the currency in which the import/export operation is denominated, although it is advisable that they coincide.

Interest Rate

The reference interest rate will depend on the currency in which the operation is financed.

Exchange Rate Risk

When the currency of Import Financing and the one from which the Importer will obtain funds from the proceeds of their sales do not coincide, an exchange rate risk is generated before which the Importer can act in two ways: assume it or cover it by contracting an Exchange Rate Insurance or a Currency Option.

Guarantees

Description

By guarantee is understood any form of guarantee by which a person undertakes to pay for a third party in case they do not fulfill the obligations they have contracted with the beneficiary.

Data that an international guarantee must include

Required Information
  • Name and address of the guarantor.
  • Name and address of the principal or applicant.
  • Name and address of the beneficiary.
  • Reason for which the guarantee is issued.
  • Currency and guaranteed amount.
  • Maturity or term for which the guarantee is issued.
  • Conditions and terms under which the execution of the guarantee should be requested.
  • The legislation applicable to the guarantee(*).
  • Other data that may be considered specifically of interest.
Recommendation

(*) It is advisable to subject on-demand guarantees or, where appropriate, counter-guarantees in favor of a local bank, or standby letters of credit to the rules of the International Chamber of Commerce (URDG 758, UCP 600, ISP 98, as appropriate) as they contribute to defining more securely the rules of the game between issuers and users of this type of guarantees.
