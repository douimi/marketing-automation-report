Export Financing/Advances

Advanced credit solutions for Spanish exporters

Overview

A specialized credit operation enabling banks to advance predetermined amounts in Euros or any officially quoted currency to Spanish exporters, facilitating collection of deferred payment sales to foreign importers.

Financial Institution
Spanish Exporter
Foreign Importer
Critical Requirement: Financing must always be linked to an export operation.

Operation Process

1

Commercial Agreement

Establishment of purchase-sale agreement between Spanish Exporter and foreign Importer

2

Goods Delivery

Spanish exporter delivers goods with future payment commitment

3

Financial Advance

Financial Institution advances operation amount in Euros or Foreign Currency

4

Payment Collection

Upon maturity, Importer pays the purchase amount

5

Advance Repayment

Exporter repays advance with Importer reimbursement

Key Characteristics

Contract Term

Term typically coincides with payment deferral granted to importer:

Pre-financing: Bank finances manufacturing phase
Post-financing (Advance): Bank advances after shipment (typically ≤ 90 days)

Amount

Maximum financing equals goods value plus expenses (transport, insurance, etc.) initially borne by Exporter but reimbursed by Importer.

Currency

Available in any currency with official quotation. Currency alignment with export operation recommended to minimize exchange rate risk.

Interest Rate

Reference rate depends on financing currency denomination.

Exchange Rate Risk Management

Currency misalignment between financing and reimbursement creates exchange rate risk. Exporters can assume risk or mitigate through Exchange Rate Insurance or Currency Options.

Forfaiting: Integrated Financing & Credit Insurance

A non-recourse financing solution allowing financial institutions to offer exporters the ability to sell commercial credits without recourse. In insolvency cases, banks cannot claim amounts from exporters.

Key Benefits

Collection guarantee assurance
Exchange & interest rate risk elimination
Political & commercial risk mitigation
Immediate liquidity provision
Off-balance sheet positioning
Enhanced sales terms flexibility

Import Financing

Credit solutions for import payment operations

Overview

Credit operation financing the period necessary for marketing and collection of imported products, enabling importers to manage cash flow and secure better purchase terms.

Insufficient funds for cash payments
Securing improved purchase prices
Financial Institution
Spanish Importer
Foreign Exporter
Critical Requirement: Financing must always be linked to import payment operations.

Operation Process

1

Commercial Agreement

Purchase/sale agreement between Spanish Importer and foreign Exporter

2

Goods Delivery

Foreign Exporter delivers goods to Spanish Importer

3

Financing Request

Spanish Importer requests bank financing for exporter payment

4

Payment Processing

Importer's Bank pays the foreign Exporter

5

Financing Repayment

Spanish Importer repays financing from sales proceeds

Import Financing Characteristics

Contract Term

Aligns with marketing and collection period for imported products. Typically ≤ 90 days, varying by product type.

Amount

Up to 100% of operation value, potentially higher for F.O.B. Incoterm purchases.

Currency

Available in any officially quoted currency. Currency alignment with import/export operation recommended.

Interest Rate

Reference rate determined by financing currency.

Exchange Rate Risk in Import Financing

Currency misalignment between financing and sales proceeds creates exchange rate risk. Importers can assume risk or hedge through Exchange Rate Insurance or Currency Options.

International Guarantees

Comprehensive risk management and payment assurance

Definition

Any form of guarantee whereby one party undertakes to pay on behalf of a third party should they fail to fulfill contracted obligations with the beneficiary.

Essential Guarantee Information

Guarantor name and address
Principal/applicant details
Beneficiary information
Guarantee issuance reason
Currency and guaranteed amount
Maturity or guarantee term
Execution conditions and terms
Applicable legislation
Additional relevant data

ICC Rules Recommendation

Subject on-demand guarantees, counter-guarantees, or standby letters of credit to International Chamber of Commerce rules (URDG 758, UCP 600, ISP 98) for enhanced security and clarity between issuers and users.
