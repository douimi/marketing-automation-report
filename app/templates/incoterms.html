What is an Incoterm®?

The Incoterms® 2020

Standardized rules addressing when risks and charges transfer from seller to buyer in international trade.

Introduced in 1936 by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC), Incoterms® provide a series of international rules to clarify trade responsibilities. The latest version, Incoterms® 2020, replaced the 2010 rules to reflect modern trade practices.

Key Changes in 2020
  • Evolution of FCA with on-board notation for bills of lading
  • New Incoterm® DPU replaces DAT
  • Differentiated insurance coverage in CIF and CIP
  • Transport organization in FCA, DAP, DPU, and DDP
Managing Risk Control

Incoterms® reduce uncertainties in international transactions by clearly defining responsibilities, risks, and costs. They help avoid litigation and dissociate risk transfer from ownership transfer, which is governed by the contract’s legal framework.

Incoterms® Clarify:
  1. Risk transfer points during shipping
  2. Shipping contract responsibilities
  3. Distribution of logistic and administrative charges
  4. Packaging, labeling, and handling duties
  5. Export/import formalities and documentation

There are 11 Incoterms® (e.g., FOB Le Havre) maintained by the ICC.

How to Use Incoterms® 2020?

Clarify in the Contract

Specify the chosen Incoterm®, port, or place in the contract, e.g., CIF Hong Kong Incoterms® 2020.

Choose Appropriate Rule

Select an Incoterm® based on your organization’s capacity, transport type, and service level.

Specify Place and Port

Assign a precise location, e.g., FCA 25 rue Saint Charles, Bordeaux, France, Incoterms® 2020.

Precautions

  • Understand each Incoterm® and its acronym
  • Use variants accurately to avoid confusion
Important Notes

Modern trade trends favor buyers by shifting logistics responsibilities to exporters. Negotiate terms carefully, especially for high-risk countries, and consider securing a letter of credit.

Sale on Departure

Goods are shipped at the buyer’s risk from the seller’s premises (EXW) or after being handed to the carrier (FCA, FAS, FOB, CFR, CIF, CPT, CIP).

Sale on Arrival

Goods are shipped at the seller’s risk until reaching the destination (DAP, DPU, DDP).

Incoterms® Definitions

EXW (Ex Works)

Seller

Prepares goods at their premises, suitably packed for export (price typically includes pallet loading).

Buyer

Bears all charges and risks from the seller’s warehouse to the destination.

Variant: EXW Loaded

Seller may handle loading goods into the buyer’s vehicle.

FCA (Free Carrier)

Seller

Loads goods at their premises (if specified) and handles export customs clearance.

Buyer

Chooses transport and carrier, paying for main transportation.

Legal Framework

Exporting Country Law

Often preferred by sellers for familiarity but may not always be optimal.

Importing Country Law

Can be less restrictive for exporters but requires thorough understanding.

Third Country Law

Neutralizes legal nationalism, often used for compromise.

Swiss Law Recommendation

Swiss law is often favored for its exporter-friendly terms and neutrality.
