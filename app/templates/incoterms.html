Incoterms® are standardized international rules that define responsibilities, costs, and risks between buyers and sellers in global trade transactions.

Introduced in 1936 by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC), these rules provide clarity and prevent misunderstandings in international trade contracts.

Key Purpose

Incoterms® clarify when risks and charges transfer from seller to buyer, helping avoid disputes and litigation in international transactions.

01

What is an Incoterm®?

Understanding the fundamentals of international trade rules

Definition & Purpose

Incoterms® are standardized rules addressing when risks and charges transfer from seller to buyer in international trade. They reduce uncertainties by clearly defining responsibilities, risks, and costs.

Key Insight: Incoterms® dissociate risk transfer from ownership transfer, which is governed by the contract's legal framework.

2020 Key Changes
  • Evolution of FCA with on-board notation for bills of lading
  • New Incoterm® DPU replaces DAT
  • Differentiated insurance coverage in CIF and CIP
  • Transport organization in FCA, DAP, DPU, and DDP
What Incoterms® Clarify
  1. Risk transfer points during shipping
  2. Shipping contract responsibilities
  3. Distribution of logistic and administrative charges
  4. Packaging, labeling, and handling duties
  5. Export/import formalities and documentation
02

How to Use Incoterms® 2020

Practical guidelines for proper implementation

Clarify in Contract

Specify the chosen Incoterm®, port, or place in the contract, e.g., CIF Hong Kong Incoterms® 2020.

Choose Appropriate Rule

Select an Incoterm® based on your organization's capacity, transport type, and service level requirements.

Specify Place and Port

Assign a precise location, e.g., FCA 25 rue Saint Charles, Bordeaux, France, Incoterms® 2020.

Important Precautions

Modern trade trends favor buyers by shifting logistics responsibilities to exporters. Negotiate terms carefully, especially for high-risk countries, and consider securing a letter of credit.

Essential Knowledge

Understand each Incoterm® and its acronym thoroughly. Use variants accurately to avoid confusion and disputes.

Incoterms® 2020 Overview

Complete list of all 11 Incoterms® rules

All Modes of Transport

Code Name Description
EXW Ex Works Seller makes goods available at their premises
FCA Free Carrier Seller delivers to carrier at named place
CPT Carriage Paid To Seller pays freight to named destination
CIP Carriage & Insurance Paid Seller pays freight and insurance
DAP Delivered at Place Seller delivers at named place ready for unloading
DPU* Delivered at Place Unloaded Seller delivers and unloads at named place
DDP Delivered Duty Paid Seller delivers with paid duties and taxes

* New for Incoterms® 2020

Maritime & Inland Waterway

Code Name Description
FAS Free Alongside Ship Seller delivers alongside ship at named port
FOB Free On Board Seller delivers on board the vessel
CFR Cost and Freight Seller pays freight to named destination port
CIF Cost, Insurance, Freight Seller pays freight and minimum insurance
Sale on Departure

Goods are shipped at the buyer's risk from the seller's premises (EXW) or after being handed to the carrier (FCA, FAS, FOB, CFR, CIF, CPT, CIP).

Sale on Arrival

Goods are shipped at the seller's risk until reaching the destination (DAP, DPU, DDP).

Key Incoterms® Definitions

Detailed explanations of commonly used Incoterms®

EXW
Ex Works
Seller

Prepares goods at their premises

Buyer

Bears all charges and risks from seller's warehouse

Seller

Buyer

Chooses transport and pays main transportation

Buyer
